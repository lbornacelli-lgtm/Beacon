About FPREN

Built for Florida's
Emergency Landscape

FPREN is an automated, AI-enhanced public radio emergency broadcasting platform developed at the University of Florida. Designed to bridge the gap between federal alert infrastructure and last-mile community delivery, FPREN monitors NWS and IPAWS feeds continuously and transforms raw alert data into clear, broadcast-ready audio within minutes.

The platform operates across 9 geographically distinct Florida broadcast zones — from the Panhandle to the Florida Keys — ensuring that emergency messaging reaches the populations most directly at risk. Each zone streams independently, enabling granular targeting of tornado warnings, hurricane advisories, flood alerts, and more.

Powered by LLaMA 3.3 70B for script generation, Piper TTS and ElevenLabs for synthesis, and a full R/Shiny monitoring dashboard, FPREN represents a new standard for public emergency communication infrastructure.

Real-Time Response

NWS and IPAWS feeds polled every 2 minutes. Critical alerts trigger immediate TTS broadcast interruption across all affected zones.

Zone-Targeted Delivery

9 Icecast streams mapped to Florida's distinct geographic regions. Alerts are routed only to the zones whose counties are listed in the NWS event.

AI-Enhanced Broadcasting

LLaMA 3.3 70B running on UF's LiteLLM gateway generates natural-language broadcast scripts tailored to alert severity, affected area, and population context.

Workflow

From Alert to Airwaves
in Under Two Minutes

FPREN's fully automated pipeline ingests federal alert data, classifies it with AI, synthesizes audio, and routes it to the appropriate zone streams — with zero manual intervention.

1

Alert Ingestion

NWS CAP & IPAWS feeds polled every 2 minutes. New alerts extracted and queued for processing.

2

AI Classification

LLaMA 3.3 70B classifies alert severity, identifies affected counties, and assesses population impact.

3

Script Generation

AI drafts a clear, professional broadcast script calibrated to alert type, severity, and audience.

4

TTS Synthesis

Piper TTS or ElevenLabs converts the script to broadcast-quality audio and saves to the zone queue.

5

Zone Routing

Alert-affected counties are mapped to Icecast zones. Audio is queued at Priority 1 for immediate playback.

6

Live Broadcast

Icecast serves the alert audio to all listeners in affected zones. Dashboard confirms broadcast completion.
